# Admin Audit to Paid Offer Routing

The Admin Audit must end with one recommended next action, not a generic list of problems.

## Routing rule

Choose the department with the highest combination of:

- repeated operating failure;
- measurable commercial impact;
- usable starting records;
- available client decision-maker;
- workflow that TAD can safely operate.

Do not recommend multiple implementations at once for the first engagement.

## Sales Admin

Recommend **Sales Admin Setup and Managed Follow-up** when the audit finds:

- leads are unowned;
- first responses are slow;
- quotes have no next action;
- follow-ups are overdue;
- quote outcomes are unknown;
- the owner manually checks several channels.

Evidence required before proposal:

- at least 10 active leads or open quotes;
- one baseline measure;
- owner or manager available for decisions;
- authorised usable records.

## Invoice Admin

Recommend Invoice Admin only when the dominant problem is supplier/customer invoice handling, missing documents, approvals or due-date control. Do not route debt collection, accounting advice or payment execution into the Sales Admin offer.

## Client Admin

Recommend Client Admin when onboarding, agreements, document collection, handovers or client requests are the dominant failure.

## Property Admin

Recommend Property Admin when maintenance requests, contractors, approvals and recurring property obligations are the dominant failure.

## Practice Admin

Recommend Practice Admin when bookings, confirmations, documents and session follow-up are the dominant failure. Sensitive or health-related information requires a separate risk review before any implementation.

## Member Admin

Recommend Member Admin when applications, renewals, member documents and recurring follow-up are the dominant failure.

## No-offer outcomes

Do not propose implementation when:

- the business cannot identify an accountable decision-maker;
- records are unavailable or unusable;
- authority to process the records is unclear;
- the workflow depends mainly on regulated professional judgement;
- the client expects autonomous external action outside the agreed boundary;
- the problem is too small to justify the service.

## Audit output

Every completed audit must produce:

1. diagnosed department;
2. current workflow failure;
3. baseline evidence;
4. recommended implementation;
5. scope boundary;
6. once-off setup price;
7. monthly service option;
8. accept, decline or revisit date.

## First-offer decision

For the current launch phase, route qualified Sales Admin findings to:

- Admin Audit: R1,500;
- Sales Admin Setup: R4,900;
- Managed Follow-up: R3,900/month.

These are launch price hypotheses. Record objections, accepted amounts, operator time and value evidence after every proposal.
