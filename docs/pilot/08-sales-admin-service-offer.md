# Sales Admin Setup and Managed Follow-up

## Positioning

The Admin Department installs and runs the follow-up system a service business is missing. DueToday is the shared control platform used to organise records, surface work due today, capture outcomes, route approvals and prove what changed.

This is a managed service, not a self-service CRM subscription.

## Ideal client

A founder-led service business that:

- receives enquiries through WhatsApp, email, phone, referrals or forms;
- sends quotes manually;
- has at least 10 active leads or open quotes;
- loses track of follow-ups or ownership;
- cannot justify hiring a full internal sales administrator;
- has an owner or manager available for decisions.

## Entry offer: Admin Audit

**Launch price hypothesis: R1,500 once-off.**

The fee is credited against the implementation when the client proceeds within 14 days.

Deliverables:

- workflow interview;
- current-state map;
- baseline leakage measures;
- highest-priority admin department;
- implementation recommendation;
- written scope and price.

## Core implementation: Sales Admin Setup

**Launch price hypothesis: R4,900 once-off.**

Includes:

- one managed client workspace;
- one Sales Admin workflow installation;
- cleanup and import of up to 50 active records;
- status and owner mapping;
- next-action and due-date rules;
- approval rules;
- follow-up templates;
- client and operator access setup;
- baseline capture;
- one implementation review;
- 14-day stabilisation period.

Not included:

- historical database migration beyond the agreed active set;
- automatic WhatsApp or email sending;
- accounting implementation;
- custom integrations;
- sales calls performed in the client's name without separate authority;
- legal, tax, credit-control or debt-collection advice.

## Ongoing service: Managed Follow-up

**Launch price hypothesis: R3,900 per month.**

Designed for up to 100 active records and one operating workflow.

Includes:

- business-day queue review;
- record ownership and next-action control;
- follow-up preparation;
- outcome and next-date capture;
- blocker escalation;
- Service Desk approvals;
- weekly service report;
- monthly workflow review;
- limited record cleanup and maintenance.

Fair-use boundary:

- one business;
- one Sales Admin workflow;
- up to 100 active records;
- up to two client decision-makers;
- no more than five material approval requests per week;
- external messages remain human-approved unless a separate authorised integration is installed.

## Additional work

Quoted separately:

- additional department workflow;
- additional business or branch;
- more than 100 active records;
- major historical cleanup;
- custom integrations;
- document extraction;
- outbound calling;
- after-hours service;
- regulated or sensitive-data workflows.

## Commercial rules

1. Setup starts only after scope, authority and payment are confirmed.
2. The client remains responsible for commercial decisions, quote accuracy and commitments made to customers.
3. TAD records actions and outcomes but does not fabricate activity or results.
4. Important decisions use the Service Desk approval process.
5. Monthly service is reviewed after the first 30 days.
6. These launch prices are hypotheses to test against real operator time, client value and willingness to pay; they are not presented as market facts.

## Success promise

TAD does not promise revenue. It promises an installed, operated and evidenced follow-up system in which active records have clear ownership, next actions, due dates, outcomes and escalation.
